\part{Applications, Open Problems, and the Road Ahead}

The final part applies the toolkit to specific phenomena and maps the research frontier. Chapter~16 examines Zipf's law through the lens of Pitman--Yor processes, combinatorial growth arguments, and the temperature window. Chapter~17 introduces Szpankowski's analytic information theory --- motif statistics, trie depth, Mellin transforms --- as the classical toolkit for sequence statistics that the LLM community should be reaching for. Chapters~18 and~19 are the strategic core: ten precisely stated open problems at the intersection of analytic combinatorics and LLMs, followed by a research roadmap that ranks them by tractability and proposes a concrete three-phase programme for a student entering the field.
