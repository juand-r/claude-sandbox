# Cybernetics & Agents Research Plan

## Goal
Deep literature review across three themes to understand the relationship between classical cybernetics and modern AI agent systems.

## Three Research Streams

### Stream 1: Original Cybernetics Sources
- Norbert Wiener's foundational work (feedback, control, communication)
- W. Ross Ashby (requisite variety, homeostasis, Design for a Brain)
- Stafford Beer (viable system model, management cybernetics)
- Heinz von Foerster (second-order cybernetics, observer)
- Gregory Bateson (ecology of mind, cybernetic epistemology)
- Warren McCulloch & Walter Pitts (logical calculus of nervous activity)
- Gordon Pask (conversation theory, interaction of actors)
- Key concepts: feedback loops, homeostasis, requisite variety, self-organization, autopoiesis, regulation, black box

### Stream 2: Modern AI Agents Literature
- LLM-based agents (ReAct, Toolformer, AutoGPT, etc.)
- Multi-agent systems (classical and modern)
- Agent architectures (BDI, cognitive architectures, tool-use)
- Autonomous agents survey papers
- Agent evaluation and benchmarks
- Memory, planning, and tool-use in agents
- Orchestration frameworks

### Stream 3: Bridging Work
- Cybernetic approaches in AI/ML
- Control theory in RL and agent design
- Self-regulating systems and adaptive agents
- Autopoiesis and artificial life connections
- Viable system model applied to software/AI
- Enactivism and embodied cognition in AI
- Feedback mechanisms in modern agent loops

## Process
- Each stream produces an independent report
- Final synthesis report combines all three
- Reports saved in this directory
