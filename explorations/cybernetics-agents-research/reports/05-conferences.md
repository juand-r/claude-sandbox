# Report: Conference Notes

**Scope:** 10 notes covering cybernetics conferences and historical events (notes/conferences/)

## What Was Covered

- **ASC (American Society for Cybernetics)** — 2023 presentations, 2024 60th anniversary
- **EMCSR (European Meeting on Cybernetics and Systems Research)** — history and status
- **IEEE SMC (Systems, Man, and Cybernetics)** — 2023 Honolulu, 2024 Kuching, 2025 Vienna

- **Mechanisation of Thought Processes (1958)** — NPL conference

## Key Findings

### 1. The Cybernetics Community Is Aware of the AI Connection But Not Engaging Technically

ASC 2023-2024 presentations include titles like "Cybernetic alternatives to current AI approaches" and "AI-Generated Art: Steering Through Noisy Channels." Paul Pangaro has been actively presenting on cybernetics and AI. But the engagement is largely philosophical and programmatic — "AI should look at cybernetics" — rather than technical. There are no conference papers that do the formal bridge work (applying variety calculus to tool use, doing stability analysis of agent loops, mapping VSM onto frameworks).

### 2. IEEE SMC Has Drifted Far From Cybernetics

The IEEE Systems, Man, and Cybernetics society now publishes primarily on: multi-agent reinforcement learning, consensus algorithms for networked systems, event-triggered control, and cyber-physical security. The "cybernetics" in the name is historical. The papers use control theory formalism (Lyapunov stability, consensus protocols) but don't engage with the broader cybernetic tradition (VSM, second-order cybernetics, autopoiesis). There is, however, useful formal work on multi-agent coordination, consensus, and resilient control that could inform agent design.

### 3. The Macy Conferences Reveal the Original Cybernetics-AI Split

The detailed Macy Conference notes show the tensions that eventually split cybernetics from AI. McCulloch and Pitts' logical calculus of neural nets was the common ancestor. The split came over methodology: the AI camp (led by McCarthy, Minsky, Simon, Newell) chose symbolic representation and search; the cybernetics camp (Ashby, Beer, von Foerster, Bateson) chose feedback, self-organization, and circular causality. The 1956 Dartmouth Conference formalized the AI side; cybernetics never had an equivalent founding moment for the feedback-based approach.

**Relevance today:** LLM agents are in some ways a reconciliation — they use neural networks (McCulloch-Pitts heritage) for symbolic manipulation (GOFAI heritage) within feedback loops (cybernetics heritage). But the agent community's theoretical vocabulary comes entirely from the AI side. The cybernetic vocabulary (variety, stability, viability, homeostasis) provides tools the AI vocabulary lacks.

### 4. The 1958 NPL Conference Anticipated Modern Concerns

The "Mechanisation of Thought Processes" conference at the UK National Physical Laboratory included contributions from Turing's colleagues, and discussed: machine learning, pattern recognition, self-organizing systems, and the automation of intellectual work. Many of the concerns raised — about the limits of formal systems, the need for adaptive behavior, the role of the environment in shaping cognition — remain relevant and unresolved.

### 5. The Conference Record Shows Cybernetics Is a Living Field, Not a Dead One

Despite the common perception that cybernetics died in the 1970s, the conference record shows active communities: ASC runs monthly presentations and annual conferences, EMCSR continues in Vienna, IEEE SMC is a large society, and new conferences (like the International Conference on Second-Order Cybernetics) have appeared. The field is small but not dead. The Constructivist Foundations journal and Kybernetes continue publishing.

---

## Assessment

The conference notes reveal a gap: the cybernetics community talks about AI but doesn't do the technical bridge work, and the AI community uses cybernetic concepts (feedback, control) without knowing the cybernetic literature. IEEE SMC has the formal tools (Lyapunov stability, consensus, multi-agent control) but doesn't connect them to the broader cybernetic tradition. The technical bridge work — applying formal cybernetic tools to LLM agent design — is not being done by anyone currently.
