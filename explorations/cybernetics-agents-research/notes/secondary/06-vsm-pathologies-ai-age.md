# The VSM and Taxonomy of Organizational Pathologies in the Age of AI

(Authors not fully extracted — MDPI access restricted). (2025). "The Viable System Model and the Taxonomy of Organizational Pathologies in the Age of Artificial Intelligence (AI)." *Systems*, 13(9), 749. DOI: 10.3390/systems13090749.

Published: 29 August 2025. Part of Special Issue "CyberSystemic Transformations for Social Good."

## Summary

Applies Organizational Cybernetics (OC) and the Viable System Model (VSM), combined with Perez Rios's Taxonomy of Organizational Pathologies (TOP), to analyze risks posed by AI integration into organizations and society. The paper argues that AI introduces novel sources of complexity (variety) that threaten organizational viability, and that cybernetic frameworks provide the diagnostic and prescriptive tools needed.

## Key Arguments

1. **AI increases environmental variety.** The integration of AI into organizations dramatically increases the variety that management systems must handle — new decision modalities, data flows, automation capabilities, and failure modes.

2. **VSM as diagnostic framework.** Each of Beer's five systems can develop pathologies when AI is introduced:
   - S1: Operational units may become dependent on AI without understanding it
   - S2: Coordination mechanisms may fail when AI introduces non-transparent decision-making
   - S3: Audit and control may lose effectiveness if AI decisions are opaque
   - S4: Intelligence/adaptation may be overwhelmed by AI-generated complexity
   - S5: Policy/identity may be displaced by AI-driven optimization

3. **Perez Rios's TOP framework** categorizes organizational pathologies into structural, functional, and informational types — all of which can be exacerbated by AI.

4. **Need for cybernetic governance.** Responsible AI innovation requires matching the variety of AI systems with governance mechanisms of equal complexity (requisite variety). Current regulatory approaches may lack sufficient variety.

## Connection to Our Research

- **VSM as agent architecture diagnostic**: The pathology taxonomy could be applied to multi-agent AI systems. When an AutoGPT-style system fails, is it an S1 pathology (bad execution), S2 (poor coordination between sub-agents), S3 (inadequate monitoring), S4 (failure to adapt to environment), or S5 (misspecified goals)?
- **Variety and AI governance**: The paper's argument that AI governance must match AI variety directly applies to AI safety — supervisory systems must be at least as complex as the systems they supervise.
- **Recursive viability**: The VSM's recursive structure (viable systems contain viable systems) maps onto hierarchical agent architectures where sub-agents must each be "viable" in Beer's sense.
