# From Intelligence to Autopoiesis: Rethinking AI Through Systems Theory

## Citation
Zönnchen, B., Dzhimova, M., & Socher, G. (2025). "From Intelligence to Autopoiesis: Rethinking Artificial Intelligence Through Systems Theory." *Frontiers in Communication*. DOI: 10.3389/fcomm.2025.1585321.

## Summary

Applies Luhmann's systems theory to reframe the AI debate away from anthropocentric questions ("Can machines think?") toward a systems-theoretic analysis of LLMs as hybrid systems embedded in social communication structures. Proposes understanding LLM output as "artificial meaning production" — not independent thinking, but recursive reflection of socially shaped linguistic patterns.

## Key Arguments

1. **Classical Turing machines are not sense-making systems.** They lack self-reference and contingent selection capacity. Purely mechanical computation without autopoiesis.

2. **ANNs/LLMs are hybrid systems.** They exhibit "loosely coupled interaction with social systems" by extracting patterns from societal communication. They are neither conscious (lacking operational closure in Luhmann's sense) nor mere tools (they participate in meaning production structures).

3. **Why LLMs ≠ autopoietic systems:** They lack:
   - Genuine self-reference / operational closure
   - Capacity for re-entry (reintroducing own guiding distinction into themselves)
   - Second-order observation
   - Contingent selections from authentic alternatives

4. **Double contingency and communication:** When two systems interact, communication emerges as uncertainty-management. LLMs participate in this but asymmetrically — they reflect learned patterns rather than generating genuine contingency.

## Relevant Formalisms

- **Autopoiesis** (Maturana/Varela): Systems that produce and maintain themselves through their own operations
- **Operational closure**: Self-referential operation where thoughts stabilize through recursive differentiation
- **Sense-making**: Selection of meaningful references from an excess of possibilities (sense arises only through self-reference)
- **Re-entry** (Spencer-Brown/Luhmann): Systems reintroduce their own guiding distinction into themselves, enabling self-observation
- **Double contingency**: Events neither necessary nor random — could have unfolded differently

## Connection to Our Research

- **Autopoiesis as design criterion:** If autopoiesis requires operational closure and self-reference, current AI agents (ReAct, AutoGPT) clearly lack it. But the "hybrid system" framing is more interesting — agents that participate in meaning production without being autopoietic themselves.
- **Second-order cybernetics lens on agents:** An agent that cannot observe itself observing (no genuine re-entry) is fundamentally different from one that can. This maps onto the distinction between ReAct (first-order: observe-act) and Reflexion (approaching second-order: reflect on own performance).
- **Luhmann vs. Maturana/Varela:** Luhmann extends autopoiesis from biology to social systems (communication as autopoietic). This gives us a richer vocabulary for multi-agent systems where communication between agents could be analyzed as its own autopoietic system.
