## Context
Presented at an ASC conference alongside texts by William T. Powers (Perceptual Control Theory) and Ernst von Glasersfeld (Radical Constructivism). This positions Maturana within the broader second-order cybernetics and radical constructivist movements.

Published the same year as "Reality: The Search for Objectivity" (see companion note), this paper addresses overlapping but distinct territory: where the "Reality" paper focuses on epistemology and ethics, "Ontology of Observing" focuses on the ontological status of the observer and self-consciousness.

## Key Arguments

### 1. The Observer as Biological System
The observer is a living system -- a biological entity whose observing is a biological phenomenon. Any ontology of observing must begin with this fact. The observer does not stand outside the world being observed; the observer is constituted by the same biological processes that constitute all living systems.

### 2. Self-Consciousness as Recursive Distinction
Self-consciousness arises when an organism, operating in language, applies its operations of distinction to itself. This is a recursive process: the organism distinguishes itself distinguishing, observes itself observing. Self-consciousness requires languaging -- it cannot arise in a non-linguistic organism.

### 3. The Physical Domain of Existence
The physical domain is not a pre-given reality that we discover. It is constituted by the observer's operations of distinction. This does not mean the physical world does not exist, but that what we call "physical reality" is always the product of an observer's distinctions. The physical domain is one domain of reality among many, brought forth through specific operations.

### 4. Objectivity-in-Parenthesis (Developed Further)
Building on the framework from "Reality" (1988), Maturana argues that putting objectivity in parentheses does not destroy science or knowledge. It merely acknowledges that all knowledge is observer-dependent. Science operates within consensual domains -- its validity comes from the operational coherences agreed upon by the scientific community, not from access to an independent reality.

### 5. The Biological Foundations
Maturana traces the chain from autopoiesis to self-consciousness:
- Autopoietic organization (cell level) -> living
- Nervous system -> expanded domain of interactions
- Structural coupling with other organisms -> social domain
- Languaging -> recursive coordination of actions
- Self-consciousness -> recursive application of distinctions to the distinguisher

Each level builds on the previous. Self-consciousness is not a mysterious "extra" -- it is the natural consequence of a languaging organism applying its own distinctions to itself.

## Significance
This paper complements "Reality" by focusing specifically on what it means to be an observer. Together, the two 1988 papers constitute Maturana's fullest philosophical statement. The paper is particularly important for understanding how autopoiesis theory addresses consciousness -- not through any special mechanism, but through the recursive structure of languaging.

## Relevance to Agent Architectures
- **Self-referential systems**: An agent that can observe its own observing (meta-cognition, self-reflection) would have a form of recursive distinction-making analogous to what Maturana describes
- **Consciousness requires languaging**: Suggests that agent self-awareness (if achievable) would emerge from recursive linguistic operations, not from adding a "consciousness module"
- **Observer-constituted reality**: Agent "world models" are not representations of reality but domains of operational coherence brought forth by the agent's own operations
- **No privileged ontology**: Challenges the assumption that there is one correct way to model the world -- different agents may constitute equally valid but different domains of reality
