## Background
Timothy A. Carey is an Australian psychologist who began using MOL in the 1990s and obtained a clinical psychology doctorate primarily to test it. While working for Scotland's NHS, he used MOL exclusively with primary care patients. **A 15-month waiting list was reduced to 0 months.**

## The Three Principles of MOL

### 1. Control
People control their lives by acting to match their perceptions with their goals (reference signals). Psychological problems are problems of control — situations where the person cannot effectively control important perceptions.

### 2. Conflict
Conflict occurs when two or more control systems try to maintain incompatible reference values for the same perceptual variable. This is usually transitory, but when unresolved and chronic, it manifests as:
- Inflexible control
- Worry
- Suppressed emotion
- Self-criticism
- Rumination

These are subjective experiences of dysfunction due to disrupted control.

### 3. Reorganization
Reorganization is the innate process that can resolve conflicts. It modifies the parameters and connectivity of the control hierarchy. But it can only operate on the level of the hierarchy where the conflict exists. If the person's awareness is stuck at the level of the symptoms (the lower-level effects of the conflict), reorganization cannot access the higher-level conflicting goals.

## How MOL Works

### The Core Technique: Going Up a Level
1. The client talks about a problem.
2. The therapist listens for **disruptions** — moments when the client's attention shifts, they pause, change topic, laugh, look away, or show any sign of a background thought intruding.
3. The therapist asks about the disruption: "What were you just thinking?" or "What just went through your mind?"
4. This redirects the client's awareness to a higher level of the hierarchy — toward the goals and values that are generating the conflicting lower-level goals.
5. The process repeats: as the client talks at the higher level, the therapist watches for further disruptions and asks about those.
6. Eventually, the client's awareness reaches the level where the conflicting goals originate, and reorganization can operate there to resolve the conflict.

### What the Therapist Does NOT Do
- No diagnosis
- No proposed solutions or remedies
- No specific techniques or protocols
- No homework assignments
- No treatment plans
- No fixed session schedule (clients schedule sessions when they want them)

The therapist's only job is to facilitate the client's awareness moving up the hierarchy. The client's own reorganization process does the actual therapeutic work.

### A-Diagnostic Nature
MOL is not transdiagnostic (applicable across diagnoses) — it is **a-diagnostic** (has no dependency on diagnostic categories at all). The mechanism (unresolved conflict → move awareness up the hierarchy → reorganization) is the same regardless of what the symptoms look like.

## Significance for Agent Design

### The "Going Up a Level" Principle
When an agent encounters a conflict or failure, the MOL approach suggests:
1. Don't try to fix the problem at the level where it manifests.
2. Instead, shift attention to the higher-level goals that are generating the conflicting sub-goals.
3. "Reorganize" at the higher level — reconsider the priorities, values, or assumptions that created the conflict.

Example: An agent is asked to "be helpful" and "be honest." A user asks a question where the helpful answer is misleading. The conflict manifests at the output level (what words to produce). But the resolution requires going up a level: What does "helpful" mean in this context? What does "honest" mean? The higher-level understanding resolves the lower-level conflict.

### Conflict Detection in Agent Systems
MOL suggests that psychological distress = chronic unresolved conflict. By analogy, "agent distress" (poor performance, incoherent outputs, contradictory behavior) might be diagnosable as unresolved conflict between reference signals. An agent introspection system could:
1. Monitor for signs of conflicting goals (oscillating outputs, hedging, contradictory statements)
2. Identify the level of the hierarchy where the conflict originates
3. "Reorganize" at that level (adjust priorities, clarify values)

### Client-Led = User-Led
MOL is radically client-led. The therapist doesn't impose structure. Applied to agents: the most effective agents might be those that don't impose their own structure on the interaction but instead follow the user's awareness, facilitating the user's own problem-solving.
