# Biology of Language: The Epistemology of Reality (1978)

## Citation
Maturana, H.R. (1978). "Biology of Language: The Epistemology of Reality." In Miller, G.A. & Lenneberg, E. (Eds.), *Psychology and Biology of Language and Thought: Essays in Honor of Eric Lenneberg*, pp. 27-63. Academic Press, New York.

## Availability
- PDF at Evergreen State College: https://sites.evergreen.edu/arunchandra/wp-content/uploads/sites/395/2018/05/BofLanguage.pdf (image-based PDF)

## Context
Maturana opens by declaring "I am not a linguist, I am a biologist" and states he will "speak about language as a biologist." This paper is Maturana's first major treatment of language from the autopoietic perspective, predating the fuller development of "languaging" in later works.

## Key Arguments

### 1. Language as Biological Phenomenon
Language is not a system of symbols or rules. It is a biological phenomenon arising from the structural coupling of organisms. Maturana approaches language not through syntax, semantics, or pragmatics, but through the biology of the organisms that use it.

### 2. The Nervous System as Closed
The nervous system is defined as a closed network of interacting neurons. Changes in neuronal activity always lead to other changes in neuronal activity within the network. With respect to its dynamics of states, the nervous system is a closed system. This is a restatement from Biology of Cognition but here applied specifically to the problem of language.

### 3. Structure-Determined Systems
Structure-determined systems do not undergo instructive interactions. Any description of an interaction in terms of "instructions" or "information transfer" is at best metaphorical. The environment triggers changes in the organism, but the organism's structure determines what those changes are.

### 4. Consensual Domains
When two or more structurally coupled organisms engage in recurrent interactions, their behaviors become mutually triggering and interlocked. From an observer's perspective, this domain of interlocked conduct is indistinguishable from a consensual domain. Language arises within consensual domains.

The operational effectiveness of structurally coupled organisms is established during their history of interactions. It is not pre-given. The domain of interactions constitutes a network of mutually triggering interlocked conducts -- this IS a consensual domain.

### 5. Language and Autopoiesis
Language is not an instrument for transmitting information about a pre-existing world. It is part of the organism's way of maintaining its autopoietic organization through social structural coupling. Words do not denote objects in a mind-independent world; they are distinctions within consensual domains.

### 6. The Epistemology of Reality
Since the nervous system is closed and organisms are structure-determined, "reality" as an observer-independent given is inaccessible. What we call reality is constituted through our distinctions in language. This is not solipsism (the physical world exists and constrains us), but it means that what we know is always mediated by our biological organization.

## Significance
This paper bridges Maturana's earlier neuroscience work and his later development of "languaging." It is the key text for understanding how autopoiesis theory extends from cellular biology to human language and communication. The 1978 paper in *Wahrnehmung und Kommunikation* (see companion note) covers similar territory for a German audience.

## The "Cognition" Chapter (1978)
Maturana also published a chapter titled "Cognition" in: Hejl, P.M., Köck, W.K. & Roth, G. (Eds.), *Wahrnehmung und Kommunikation*, pp. 29-49. Peter Lang, Frankfurt, 1978. This chapter was presented at a symposium "Wahrnehmung und Kommunikation -- interdisziplinäre Aspekte einer Theorie selbstreferentieller (kognitiver) Systeme" at University of Bremen, March 30 - April 2, 1977.

Key ideas from the "Cognition" chapter (per secondary sources):
- Observers operate in a consensual domain and cannot exist outside of these domains
- Every statement by an observer is necessarily consensual
- The primary operation of an observer consists of making distinctions
- Autopoietic systems are relativistic with respect to (1) the person of the observer, (2) the history of the observer, and (3) the interactions of observers

## Relevance to Agent Architectures
- **Consensual domains** map to shared interaction protocols between agents -- language emerges from interaction history, not from pre-given rules
- **Structure-determined interpretation** maps to how different LLMs interpret the same prompt differently based on their training (structure)
- **No information transfer** challenges the standard model of agent communication as message-passing
- **Language as coordination** suggests agent communication should be understood as mutual behavioral coordination, not information exchange
