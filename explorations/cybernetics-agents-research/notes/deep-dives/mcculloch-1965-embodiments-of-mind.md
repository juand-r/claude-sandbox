# McCulloch — *Embodiments of Mind* (MIT Press, 1965)

**Author:** Warren S. McCulloch
**Publisher:** MIT Press, Cambridge, MA, 1965
**Reissued:** 2016, with new foreword and biographical essay by Michael Arbib
**ISBN:** 978-0-262-52961-7
**Available:** [MIT Press](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262529617/embodiments-of-mind/), [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/embodimentsofmin0000mccu)

## What It Is

A collection of 21 essays and lectures by McCulloch, spanning his career. Not a monograph but a curated anthology of his most important work. Includes philosophical essays, technical papers, and even sonnets.

## Significance

This is the single most important collection for understanding McCulloch's thought. It brings together the full arc of his work:
- From pure logic (1943) to pattern recognition (1947)
- From neural theory to philosophy of mind
- From technical papers to poetry

The title itself is the thesis: mind is not separate from body. Mind is *embodied* — it exists in the physical structure and activity of neural nets. There are no disembodied ideas; there are only ideas immanent in nervous activity (echoing the 1943 paper title).

## Introduction by Seymour Papert (1965 edition)

The original edition included an introduction by Seymour Papert (later famous for Logo and constructionism in education). Papert was a student of Piaget and a colleague of Minsky at MIT — placing him at the intersection of developmental psychology, education, and AI.

## Biographical Essay by Michael Arbib (2016 edition)

The reissued edition includes a biographical essay by Michael Arbib, who was McCulloch's research assistant. Arbib went on to become a major figure in computational neuroscience.
