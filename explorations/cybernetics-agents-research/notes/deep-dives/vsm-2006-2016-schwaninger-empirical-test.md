# Markus Schwaninger — VSM Empirical Testing and Applications

## Author Profile

Institute of Management, University of St. Gallen, Switzerland. Leading empirical researcher on VSM validity.

## Key Papers

### 1. Schwaninger (2006) — "Design for Viable Organizations: The Diagnostic Power of the Viable System Model"
- **Journal:** Kybernetes, Vol. 35, No. 7/8, pp. 955-966
- **Purpose:** Empirically test whether VSM specifies necessary and sufficient preconditions for viability
- **Method:** Multiple case studies across diverse domains
- **Finding:** Evidence corroborates the model's claim. "Enormous diagnostic power."
- **Case:** Health resort company K&K in Bad Rappenau — strategic restructuring guided by VSM in face of regulatory change.

### 2. Schwaninger & Scheef (2016) — "A Test of the Viable System Model: Theoretical Claim vs. Empirical Evidence"
- **Journal:** Cybernetics and Systems, Vol. 47, No. 7, pp. 544-569
- **Purpose:** Broader empirical test via survey and quantitative analysis
- **Key claim tested:** VSM asserts to specify *necessary and sufficient* preconditions for viability of *any* organisation
- **Method:** Survey-based quantitative analysis (not just case studies)
- **Result:** 71% correlation between organisational viability (as measured by VSM criteria) and survivability of existential crises
- **Significance:** This is the strongest quantitative test of VSM's empirical claims to date.

### 3. Schwaninger (1990) — "Embodiments of Organizational Fitness: The VSM as a Guide"
- **Journal:** Systems Practice, Vol. 3, pp. 249-264
- Synthesis of VSM and multilevel concept of organizational fitness
- Argues established models of organizational control are insufficient for growing environmental complexity

### 4. Schwaninger (2009) — Intelligent Organizations: Powerful Models for Systemic Management
- **Publisher:** Springer (2nd ed.)
- Book-length treatment of VSM and related models
- Contains detailed VSM literature review

### 5. Schwaninger & Pérez Ríos (2010) — "Models of Organizational Cybernetics for Diagnosis and Design"
- **Journal:** Kybernetes, Vol. 39, No. 9-10, pp. 1529-1550
- Joint paper combining Schwaninger's empirical approach with Pérez Ríos' methodological framework

## Key Findings Across Schwaninger's Work

1. **VSM's diagnostic claim is empirically supported** — no case study has been found where VSM diagnosis was incorrect
2. **Quantitative validation** — the 71% correlation figure is the strongest empirical number in VSM research
3. **Cross-domain applicability** — cases cover manufacturing, healthcare, education, government
4. **The "necessary and sufficient" claim** — Schwaninger treats this as a testable scientific hypothesis, not just a philosophical position

## Limitations Noted

- Sample sizes in quantitative work are modest
- Self-selection bias in case studies (organisations that adopt VSM may already be more systems-literate)
- The "sufficient" part of the claim is harder to test than the "necessary" part
