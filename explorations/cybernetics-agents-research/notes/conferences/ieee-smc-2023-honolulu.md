# IEEE SMC 2023 — Honolulu, Hawai'i (October 1–4, 2023)

**Theme:** "Improving the Quality of Life"
**Venue:** Sheraton Waikiki, Oahu
**Attendance:** ~600 in-person, 350+ online presentations
**Proceedings:** IEEE Xplore (as of January 2024)

## Plenary Talks

### 1. Professor Tachi — Telexistence
- Vision of a society where humans "exist" and operate at a different physical or virtual location.
- **Relevant:** Telepresence as human-machine symbiosis, extending agency through technology.

### 2. Professor Hadaegh — Distributed Spacecraft Systems
- Multiple, distributed spacecraft systems. Transformative revolution in scientific exploration.
- **Relevant:** Multi-agent coordination in physical systems (space).

### 3. Professor Maja Matarić — Personalized Human-Robot Interaction
- How robots can improve quality of life for users who need assistance.
- Personalization implies adaptation — cybernetic feedback loops in robot behavior.
- **Relevant:** Adaptive agent design for human needs.

### 4. Professor Francisco Herrera — Trust in Artificial Intelligence
- Trust as essential for AI in healthcare, government, justice, daily life.
- **Relevant:** Trust as a cybernetic constraint on agent deployment. Echoes Ashby: without trust, the system cannot achieve requisite variety in its interactions.

## Conference Structure

The Cybernetics track included topics: Agent-Based Modeling, AIoT, AI and Applications, among many others.

Papers were peer-reviewed and published as archival publications in IEEE Xplore.

## Assessment

SMC 2023 shows the cybernetics community increasingly framing its traditional concerns (control, communication, adaptation) through the lens of AI and robotics. Matarić's personalized HRI work and Herrera's trust-in-AI work are particularly relevant to our cybernetics-agents bridge:
- **Personalization** = adaptive control = feedback = cybernetics
- **Trust** = observer-inclusion = second-order concern
