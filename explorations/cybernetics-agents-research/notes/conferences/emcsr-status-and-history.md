# EMCSR — European Meetings on Cybernetics and Systems Research

## Status

**The EMCSR appears to be inactive or on hiatus since 2018/2020.** No evidence of EMCSR editions in 2022, 2024, or beyond was found despite extensive searching.

## History

- **Founded:** 1972 by Robert Trappl, Vienna, Austria
- **Frequency:** Biennial
- **Organization:** Originally by Austrian Society for Cybernetic Studies (OSGK); since 2012 by Bertalanffy Center for the Study of Systems Science (BCSSS)
- **2016:** Reinvented as "emcsr avantgarde" — a "talent scout event" in Systems Science with competition format judged by international scientific jury
- **2018:** Co-organized with European Union for Systemics in Brussels
- **2020:** Postponed due to COVID pandemic; organizers expressed hope for 2022
- **2022+:** No evidence of resumption found

## EMCSR 2016 — "FutureVision?! Which life do we want?"

**Dates:** March 30 – April 1, 2016
**Venue:** Sigmund Freud Private University Vienna

### Program Structure
- Day 1: Leadership, Governance & Society
- Day 2: Transformation & Systems Design

### Format
- Satellite workshops on specific topics organized by established researchers.
- Competition for "The Ludwig von Bertalanffy Young Scientist Award."
- Selected researchers are nominees; international jury evaluates submissions.

- Stefan Blachfellner (BCSSS)

## EMCSR Scope

Open to any researcher in cybernetics-adjacent fields:
- Systems biology, information systems
- Complex adaptive systems, sociocybernetics
- Evolutionary economics, ecosystems research
- Systems philosophy, constructivism
- Self-organization, cognitive science
- Network theory, artificial intelligence
- Any specialization rooted in cybernetics, systems theory, or complexity theory

## The Bertalanffy Center (BCSSS)

- Founded 2004 in Vienna
- Accredited by European Union
- Member of International Federation for Systems Research (IFSR)
- Advances General System Theory (GST) as founded by Ludwig von Bertalanffy
- Hosts EMCSR since 2012

## Alternative: EUMAS (European Conference on Multi-Agent Systems)

Since EMCSR appears inactive, the European Conference on Multi-Agent Systems (EUMAS) may be a better venue to track:
- **EUMAS 2024:** August 26–28, 2024, University College Dublin, Ireland
- **EUMAS 2025:** Announced
- Focus: Multi-agent systems, which is more directly relevant to our AI agents research than EMCSR's broader systems science scope.

## Assessment for Our Research

EMCSR's apparent inactivity since 2018/2020 limits its utility for finding recent papers connecting cybernetics to agent design. The conference historically provided a meeting point for second-order cybernetics, constructivism, and systems design — exactly the theoretical traditions we're drawing on. Its absence leaves a gap in the European landscape.

The BCSSS continues to operate and may publish relevant work through other channels. The OSGK's founding of OFAI (Austrian Research Institute for Artificial Intelligence) in 1984 is a historical footnote showing the cybernetics-AI institutional connection.
