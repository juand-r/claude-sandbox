# IEEE SMC 2025 — Vienna, Austria (October 5–8, 2025)

**Theme:** "Navigating Frontiers: Smart Systems for a Dynamic World"
**Scale:** 2,100+ submissions from ~60 countries, ~1,200 accepted papers
**Venue:** Austria Center Vienna (ACV)

## Keynote Talks

### 1. Peng Shi (University of Adelaide, Australia)
**"From Model-Based to AI-Empowered Cyber-Physical Multi-Agent Systems"**
- Monday October 6, 09:30–10:30
- **Directly relevant to our research.**
- Shi is Editor-in-Chief of IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics, VP of IEEE SMC Society, received Norbert Wiener Award (2024).
- Cyber-physical multi-agent systems (CPMAS) integrate autonomous systems and human participants through cyber-layer communication and physical-layer collaboration.
- Traditional approach: model-based control for robust coordination. New approach: AI-empowered (RL, deep neural networks, bio-inspired AI).
- Addresses challenges of integrating model-driven and AI-driven strategies: stability, safety, security.
- Applications: intelligent manufacturing, autonomous vehicles, smart infrastructure.
- **Key takeaway for us:** This is the cybernetics community's own framing of the shift from classical control to AI-based agents. The "model-based to AI-empowered" framing mirrors the first-order to second-order cybernetics transition — but in engineering practice rather than epistemology.

## Assessment for Our Research

IEEE SMC 2025 shows the cybernetics community is actively engaging with AI agents — but primarily from an **engineering control** perspective rather than the **epistemological/second-order** perspective of the ASC tradition. Key themes:
- Model-based → AI-empowered transition in multi-agent control
- Human-in-the-loop and human-robot collaboration as first-class concerns
- Trust, safety, and compliance as system design constraints
- LLM + MAS convergence is an emerging topic within the society

The gap between IEEE SMC's engineering cybernetics and ASC's theoretical cybernetics remains visible. The "From AI Agent to DT Agent" (digital twin) special session is interesting as a bridge concept.
