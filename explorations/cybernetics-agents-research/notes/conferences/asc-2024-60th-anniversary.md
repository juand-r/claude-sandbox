# ASC 2024 — 60th Anniversary Conference (June 15–19, 2024)

**Theme:** "Living Cybernetics | Playing Language"
**Location:** Washington, DC
**Duration:** 5 days (3 conference + 2 workshop days)
**Sessions:** 80+ mostly participatory sessions
**Synergy:** Co-located with ISSS conference (June 9–13) in same city; June 14 dedicated to inter-societal conversations via IFSR.

## Conference Overview

The ASC celebrated 60 years since its 1964 founding (first president: Warren McCulloch). The theme "Living Cybernetics | Playing Language" draws from the narrative of *Mimsy Were the Borogoves*, suggesting that cybernetic languaging shares a logic informed by understandings of processes of living.

## Thematic Strands

1. **Languaging in/for action**
2. **Archiving archiving** — A second-order reflexive turn: rather than simply storing voices of the past, reviving them so their wisdom becomes productive for creating desirable futures.
3. **Reimagining technicity**

## Key People & Presentations

### Paul Pangaro (ASC President, CMU)
- Listed as presenter/participant.
- As ASC President since 2021, Pangaro is co-lead of the **#NewMacy Initiative**, which focuses on "responding to the pandemic of today's AI algorithms."
- The #NewMacy goal: "bring about the integration of today's digital AI technologies with 'analogic' frameworks — humane, organic, open-ended, resonant, and socially-animated interactivity."
- Pangaro's framing: AI arose from the theoretical side of cybernetics, but AI moved away from the primacy of actions and interactions as the means of understanding and achieving intelligence.
- **This is the core cybernetic critique of current AI agents:** they lack genuine conversational capacity, genuine feedback, genuine learning-through-interaction.

### Stuart Umpleby
- No specific 2024 presentation found in search results.
- Umpleby served as ASC president 1980–1982; made honorary life fellow at 50th Anniversary (2014).
- His recent work focuses on third-order cybernetics (evolution of society) and second-order science, but **does not directly address modern AI systems** like LLMs or AI agents.
- Key Umpleby works remain relevant as context:
  - "Second-Order Cybernetics as a Fundamental Revolution in Science" (2016, Constructivist Foundations)
  - "Third Order Cybernetics as the Evolution of Society" (~2017)
  - "Recent Developments in Cybernetics, from Cognition to Social Systems" (with Medvedeva & Lepskiy)
  - "The Beginnings of Second Order Cybernetics" (2022, with Medvedeva)

## The #NewMacy Initiative (ASC, ongoing)

- Over 100 bi-weekly video meetings since inception.
- Multi-session conference engagements: RSD10 (2021), ISSS 2022, RSD11 (2022), RSD12 (October 2023, Carnegie Mellon).
- Fall 2024: "Artificial (and Othered) Intelligences" public program series at CMU Architecture.
- Goal: "create new design patterns and working prototypes" as alternatives to "Today's AI."
- The initiative frames AI as a social issue as well as a technological one.
- **This is the most significant ongoing ASC effort connecting cybernetics to AI agents.** But it's primarily a critique-and-alternative-framing effort, not an engineering one.

## Enacting Cybernetics Journal (2024)

**"Gordon Pask's Conversation Theory and Interaction of Actors Theory: Research to Practice"**
- Authors: Shantanu Tilak, Thomas Manning, Michael Glassman, Paul Pangaro, Bernard Scott
- Published in *Enacting Cybernetics* 2(1), Article 1 (2024). DOI: 10.58695/ec.11
- Three-part paper on Pask's CT and IA theory:
  1. Core concepts of CT and IA
  2. Visual representations applied to Pask's CASTE, THOUGHTSTICKER, and ThoughtShuffler
  3. Two pathways for design based on CT and IA
- Key concept: Human agents, the "epistemological laboratory" (EL), and environment can be conceptualized as parts of a homeostatic whole in technology-assisted conversation.
- Pask's aim: domain-general framework for psychologists, educators, designers, and computer scientists to understand conversations.
- **Status:** Open access at https://enacting-cybernetics.org/articles/10.58695/ec.11

## Assessment for Our Research

The ASC community operates in a very different register from IEEE SMC. Where IEEE SMC papers on multi-agent systems focus on distributed control under adversarial conditions, the ASC tradition focuses on:
- **Observer inclusion** (second-order cybernetics)
- **Conversation as the fundamental unit of intelligence** (Pask/Pangaro)
- **Ethics and agency in AI** (#NewMacy)
- **Reflexivity** — the system studying itself

The gap is clear: ASC has the philosophical framework that could inform better agent design (conversation theory, autopoiesis, second-order observation), but hasn't produced engineering papers applying these frameworks to modern LLM agents. The #NewMacy initiative is the closest bridge, but it's still at the critique/design-pattern stage rather than implementation.

**The Tilak et al. (2024) paper on Pask's CT/IA is the most actionable ASC-adjacent work for our project** — it operationalizes conversation theory for technology design.
