## Abstract/Summary
Since 1981, scientists in the United States and the Soviet Union had been meeting to discuss the foundations of cybernetics and general systems theory. Several different methods of communication were tried, and most conversations focused on epistemology, methodology, and management.

Two key ideas explored: "cognitive efficiency" and "collective intelligence."

## Key Themes
- East-West dialogue on cybernetics during the Cold War
- Different emphases: US cybernetics more biological/cognitive, Soviet more mathematical/engineering
- Cybernetics was much more mainstream in the USSR than in the US (it was taught as a university discipline)
- Epistemological foundations -- what counts as knowledge in systems science?

## Relevance to Our Research
The Soviet tradition of cybernetics was more closely tied to engineering and control, while the American tradition moved toward biology and cognition. This maps interestingly onto the current split between control-theoretic and cognitive approaches to AI agents. Also relevant: the USSR treated cybernetics as a general science of control applicable to social systems -- a perspective now resurfacing in multi-agent coordination.
