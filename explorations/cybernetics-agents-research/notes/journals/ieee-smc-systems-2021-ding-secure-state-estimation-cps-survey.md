# Secure State Estimation and Control of Cyber-Physical Systems: A Survey

**Authors:** Derui Ding (Shanghai Univ. of Science and Technology), Qing-Long Han, Xiaohua Ge (Swinburne University of Technology), Jun Wang
**Journal:** IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems, vol. 51, no. 1, pp. 176-190, January 2021
**DOI:** 10.1109/TSMC.2020.3041121
**Citations:** ~518

## Summary

Survey on secure state estimation and control for cyber-physical systems (CPS). CPS integrate physical processes with cyber infrastructure using ubiquitous computation and communication. This integration exposes systems to cyber attacks that can:
- Falsify sensor signals, disrupting state estimation
- Distort actual operational conditions
- Compromise controller integrity

Covers:
- Attack models (DoS, false data injection, replay attacks)
- Secure state estimation methods
- Secure control methodologies
- Defense strategies for CPS

## Relevance to Our Research

Highly relevant intersection of cybernetics and modern systems engineering. CPS are essentially the modern instantiation of Wiener's original cybernetic vision -- systems where information (cyber) and physical processes are tightly coupled through feedback loops.

The security angle adds a dimension that classical cybernetics did not fully address: adversarial disruption of the feedback loop itself. This connects to:
- Second-order cybernetics (the observer is part of the system, and can be compromised)
- Requisite variety (attackers must be countered with sufficient defensive variety)
- Viable System Model (VSM) -- security as part of System 4 (intelligence/adaptation)

This is one of the most-cited papers in the journal in recent years (518 citations).
