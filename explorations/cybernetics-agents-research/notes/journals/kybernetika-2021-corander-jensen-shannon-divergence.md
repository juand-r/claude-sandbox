# On the Jensen-Shannon Divergence and the Variation Distance for Categorical Probability Distributions

**Authors:** Jukka Corander, Ulpu Remes, Timo Koski
**Journal:** Kybernetika, Vol. 57, No. 6, pp. 879-907, 2021
**DOI:** 10.14736/kyb-2021-6-0879
**URL:** https://www.kybernetika.cz/content/2021/6/879
**PDF:** https://www.kybernetika.cz/content/2021/6/879/paper.pdf
**Keywords:** blended divergences, Chan-Darwiche metric, likelihood-free inference, implicit maximum likelihood, reverse Pinsker inequality, simulator-based inference

## Summary

Establishes a decomposition of the Jensen-Shannon divergence into a linear combination of a
scaled Jeffreys' divergence and a reversed Jensen-Shannon divergence. Derives tight upper and
lower bounds for JS divergence in terms of squared variation distance.

## Main Contributions

1. Novel decomposition: JS = linear combination of Jeffreys' + reversed JS
2. Upper/lower bounds via Pinsker inequality and reverse Pinsker inequality
3. Proves asymptotic equivalence of MLE and minimum JS divergence estimation
4. Application to likelihood-free simulator-based inference

## Relevance to Cybernetics-Agents Research

- **Information-theoretic foundations:** JS divergence is fundamental in information theory and
  is used in many agent/ML contexts (GANs, variational inference, model comparison)
- **Bayesian regulation connection:** the link to likelihood-free inference is relevant to
  Bayesian approaches where likelihood functions are intractable (common in complex agent systems)
- **Simulator-based inference:** agents that must infer properties of their environment from
  simulations can use these bounds
- **Divergence measures as regulation metrics:** in cybernetic terms, divergence measures
  quantify how far a system is from a target distribution -- fundamental to regulation
