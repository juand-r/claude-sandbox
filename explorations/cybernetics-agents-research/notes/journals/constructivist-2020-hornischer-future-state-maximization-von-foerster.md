# Foresight Rather than Hindsight? Future State Maximization As a Computational Interpretation of Heinz von Foerster's Ethical Imperative

**Authors:** Hannes Hornischer, Simon Plakolb, Georg Jäger, Manfred Füllsack
**Year:** 2020
**Journal:** Constructivist Foundations, Vol. 16, No. 1, pp. 36-49
**URL:** https://constructivist.info/16/1/036
**Type:** Target article

## Core Contribution

Connects von Foerster's Ethical Imperative ("Act always so as to increase the number of choices") to the AI principle of Future State Maximization (FSM). This is a direct bridge between second-order cybernetics and modern AI research.

## Key Arguments

- Von Foerster's Ethical Imperative corresponds "almost literally" to the principle of Future State Maximization in recent AI research.
- FSM operates by actively exploring potential futures that maximize available possibilities — foresight rather than hindsight.
- Traditional AI/ML methods extrapolate from historical data patterns (hindsight-oriented). FSM uses "in-action sampling of probes from possible futures."
- This approach handles non-linear and abruptly emerging developments better than traditional methods.
- Three computational investigations demonstrate FSM applications across coordination tasks.
- The principle conforms with constructivist epistemology and cybernetic principles.

## Relevance to Project

This is perhaps the most directly relevant paper for connecting von Foerster to modern AI. The FSM principle — maximizing future available states — is a concrete, implementable principle derived from cybernetic ethics. It provides an alternative to reward-maximization that is grounded in second-order cybernetics. The "foresight vs hindsight" framing is a productive way to distinguish constructivist AI from mainstream approaches.
