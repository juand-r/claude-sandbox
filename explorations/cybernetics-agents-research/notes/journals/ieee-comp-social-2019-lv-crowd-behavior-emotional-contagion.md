## Summary

Models crowd behavior evolution in political rallies using emotional contagion dynamics. Adapts the epidemiological SIR (Susceptible-Infected-Recovered) model to model how emotions spread through crowds, combined with OCEAN personality model traits (specifically extroversion and empathy). Studies how individual personality differences affect collective emotional dynamics and resulting crowd behavior.

## Key Contributions

1. **SIR model for emotional contagion**: Adapts the epidemiological SIR model to emotional spreading in crowds — individuals can be susceptible to emotional influence, "infected" with an emotion, or "recovered" (no longer emotionally reactive).

2. **Personality-modulated contagion**: Uses OCEAN personality traits (extroversion and empathy) to modulate how susceptible individuals are to emotional contagion and how strongly they transmit emotions.

3. **Political rally scenarios**: Models two abstract political rally scenarios, showing how initial conditions, personality distributions, and spatial dynamics lead to different collective behavioral outcomes.

## Relevance to Cybernetics Research

- **Self-organizing collective behavior**: Crowd behavior emerges from local interactions (emotional contagion between nearby individuals) without central coordination — a self-organizing system.
- **Contagion as positive feedback**: Emotional contagion is a positive feedback loop — emotion spreads, intensifies, spreads further. The SIR model captures how this positive feedback eventually saturates (recovery).
- **Stability and instability**: Political rallies can tip between stable and unstable states depending on personality distributions and initial emotional states — directly analogous to cybernetic stability analysis.
- **Collective intelligence / collective stupidity**: This is the dark side of collective intelligence — emotional contagion can drive crowds toward irrational behavior, a failure mode of social self-organization.
