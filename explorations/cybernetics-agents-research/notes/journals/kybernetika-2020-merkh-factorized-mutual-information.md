# Factorized Mutual Information Maximization

**Authors:** Thomas Merkh and Guido Montufar
**Journal:** Kybernetika, Vol. 56, No. 5, pp. 948-978, 2020
**DOI:** 10.14736/kyb-2020-5-0948
**URL:** https://www.kybernetika.cz/content/2020/5/948
**PDF:** https://www.kybernetika.cz/content/2020/5/948/paper.pdf
**Keywords:** multi-information, mutual information, divergence maximization, marginal specification problem, transportation polytope

## Summary

Investigates probability distributions that maximize the average multi-information across a
collection of margins. These functionals serve as computationally cheaper proxies for maximizing
the multi-information of a set of variables or the mutual information of two subsets.

## Core Problem

Uses the relationship I(X,Y) = H(X) - H(X|Y) to frame mutual information maximization.
The divergence maximization problem is considered for general classes of probability models
including probabilistic graphical models with hidden variables and stochastic neural networks.

## Key Contributions

- Characterizes maximizers of factorized multi-information
- Explores relationships to maximizers of full multi-information and mutual information
- Provides lower computational/estimation complexity alternatives
- Extends to Bregman divergence settings

## Relevance to Cybernetics-Agents Research

- **Information-theoretic control:** Mutual information maximization is central to
  information-theoretic approaches to control (e.g., empowerment, intrinsic motivation)
- **Agent perception/action:** Maximizing MI between actions and outcomes is a
  cybernetic principle for effective agency
- **Computational tractability:** The factorized approach addresses the practical problem
  that full MI computation is expensive -- important for real-time agent systems
- **Neural network connection:** Extension to stochastic neural networks bridges to
  modern deep learning-based agent architectures
