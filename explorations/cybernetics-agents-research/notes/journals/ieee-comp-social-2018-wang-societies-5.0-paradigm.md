# Societies 5.0: A New Paradigm for Computational Social Systems Research

## Summary

Editorial/perspective piece by Fei-Yue Wang (founding EIC of IEEE TCSS) introducing "Societies 5.0" as a new research paradigm. This is a programmatic statement for the field of computational social systems, laying out a vision that maps onto the ACP (Artificial systems, Computational experiments, Parallel execution) framework.

The "5.0" refers to a progression of societal stages, culminating in smart, cyber-physical-social systems where computation is deeply integrated with social processes.

## Key Contributions

1. **Societies 5.0 concept**: A vision of society where physical, cyber, and social spaces are deeply intertwined, requiring new computational methods to understand and govern.

2. **Research agenda for TCSS**: Lays out the scope of computational social systems as a field — modeling, simulation, analysis of man-man, man-machine, and machine-machine social organizations.

3. **ACP methodology as foundation**: Artificial systems + Computational experiments + Parallel execution as the methodological backbone for studying complex social systems.

## Relevance to Cybernetics Research

- This is essentially a cybernetic manifesto for social systems research. The ACP framework is deeply cybernetic: build models (artificial systems), experiment with them (computational experiments), and use feedback between model and reality (parallel execution).
- "Parallel execution" is a form of cybernetic feedback: the artificial system and real system run in parallel, each informing the other.
- The Societies 5.0 vision echoes Stafford Beer's viable system model applied at societal scale.
- Connects to governance cybernetics: how do you steer complex social systems computationally?
