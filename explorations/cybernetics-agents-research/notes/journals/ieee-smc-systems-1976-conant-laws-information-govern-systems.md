# Conant (1976) — Laws of Information which Govern Systems

## Citation
Conant, R.C. (1976). "Laws of Information which Govern Systems." IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics, SMC-6(4), 240-255.

## Key Ideas

### Information-Theoretic Laws for Systems
Conant uses a generalized version of Shannon's information theory to deduce structural properties of general systems. The paper establishes "laws" — necessary information-theoretic relationships that any system must satisfy.

### Connection to Good Regulator Theorem
Conant is co-author (with W. Ross Ashby) of the famous **Good Regulator Theorem** (1970): "Every good regulator of a system must be a model of that system." While the Good Regulator paper was published in the International Journal of Systems Science, this 1976 IEEE SMC paper extends the information-theoretic approach to analyzing system structure more broadly.

### System Structure from Information Flows
The paper's core contribution is showing how information-theoretic analysis can reveal the structure of complex systems — which components communicate with which, what information flows are necessary, and what the constraints on system organization must be.

### Earlier Related Work
Conant also published "The Information Transfer Required in Regulatory Processes" in IEEE Transactions on Systems Science and Cybernetics, SSC-5(4), 334-338, 1969 — establishing the information-theoretic foundations for regulation.

## Relevance to Agent Design
- Information-theoretic constraints on system regulation are fundamental to understanding what an agent must know/model to regulate a process effectively
- The idea that system structure can be deduced from information flow patterns is relevant to designing agent communication architectures
- Combined with the Good Regulator Theorem, this work says: an agent that successfully regulates its environment must internally model the relevant aspects of that environment. This is a formal argument for why agents need world models.
- The quantitative information-theoretic framework could potentially be used to analyze the information requirements of different agent architectures

## Context
Conant's work bridges Ashby's qualitative cybernetics with quantitative information theory. This makes it possible to move from "the regulator needs variety" (Ashby) to "the regulator needs this much information capacity" (Conant).
