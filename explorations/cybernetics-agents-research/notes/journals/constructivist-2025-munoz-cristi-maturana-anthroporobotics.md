# Steps Toward Anthroporobotics: A Conversation With Humberto Maturana

**Author:** Ignacio Munoz-Cristi
**Year:** 2025
**Journal:** Constructivist Foundations, Vol. 21, No. 1, pp. 1-12
**URL:** https://constructivist.info/21/1/001.munoz-cristi
**Type:** Target article (previously unpublished 2007 interview with Maturana)

## Core Contribution

Presents an unpublished 2007 interview with Humberto Maturana (co-creator of autopoiesis) on robotics design beyond representationalist schemes. Introduces "anthroporobotics" as a transdisciplinary field.

## Key Arguments

- Maturana explores robots capable of "languaging and emotioning, self-consciously"
- Design of artificial systems should use **closed dynamical systems** and **structural plasticity** as operative principles
- Non-representational approaches should replace traditional semantic models in robotics
- "Objectivity in parentheses" and "love as the acceptance of the other" serve as epistemic criteria
- **Structural coupling** between systems takes priority over information transfer
- Demonstrates "experiential coherences of processes" and suggests "generative mechanisms"
- Thinking about robotics and cognition beyond representationalist schemes, orienting machine design without resorting to semantic notions that "obscure processes"

## Keywords

Anthroporobotics, closed dynamics, emotion, languaging, non-representationalism, structural coupling, structural plasticity

## Relevance to Project

This is Maturana himself talking about how to build robots based on autopoietic principles. The emphasis on structural coupling over information transfer, closed dynamics over input-output models, and the rejection of representationalism — these are the foundational design principles for a genuinely cybernetic agent. The notion that robots should be capable of "languaging and emotioning" (Maturana's coined terms) pushes far beyond current AI paradigms. Published in 2025, making it very current.

Note: Maturana died in 2021; this interview from 2007 was previously unpublished.
