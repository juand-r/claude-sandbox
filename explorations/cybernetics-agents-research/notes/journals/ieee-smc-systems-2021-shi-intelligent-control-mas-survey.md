# A Survey on Intelligent Control for Multiagent Systems

**Authors:** Peng Shi, Bing Yan (University of Adelaide)
**Journal:** IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems, vol. 51, no. 1, pp. 161-175, January 2021
**DOI:** 10.1109/TSMC.2020.3042823
**Citations:** ~233

## Summary

Survey of intelligent control techniques for large-scale multiagent systems (MAS) facing dual constraints: limited interaction capabilities and system uncertainties. Categorizes published results along two axes:

### Interaction-level constraints:
- **Limited sensing-based control** - agents have incomplete local relative information
- **Event-based control** - communication triggered by events rather than continuous
- **Pinning-based control** - only a subset of agents are directly controlled

### System-level constraints:
- **Resilient control** - maintaining performance under attacks or faults
- **Collaborative control under system constraints** - handling nonlinearities, uncertainties, saturation

### Topics covered:
- Consensus problem
- Formation control
- Flocking control
- Applications in robotics, complex networks, transportation

## Relevance to Our Research

This is a good overview paper connecting intelligent control theory with MAS. The categorization by constraint type (interaction vs. system level) is a useful framework. The "resilient control" category is directly relevant to our interest in cybernetics-inspired fault tolerance.

Key insight: the paper frames MAS control as fundamentally about managing information constraints -- which maps well to Ashby's law of requisite variety and the cybernetic emphasis on information flow.
