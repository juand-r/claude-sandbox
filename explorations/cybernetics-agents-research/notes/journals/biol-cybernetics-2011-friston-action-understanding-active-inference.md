# Friston, Mattout & Kilner (2011) - Action understanding and active inference

**Journal:** Biological Cybernetics, 104, 137-160
**Authors:** Karl J. Friston, Jérémie Mattout, James Kilner
**DOI:** 10.1007/s00422-011-0424-z
**Year:** 2011
**Access:** Springer paywall. Could not access full text.

## Summary

Proposes that the mirror neuron system implements Bayes-optimal perception of actions,
whether emitted by oneself or observed in others. Uses active inference / free energy
formulation to model action understanding.

## Key Arguments

- **Mirror neurons as predictive coding:** The same generative models used to produce
  motor behavior can be deployed to predict/recognize actions of self or others.
- **Switching between action and observation:** The difference is simply in the precision
  (attention) afforded to proprioceptive signals. During action, proprioceptive predictions
  drive movement; during observation, they are attenuated.
- **Intentions as trajectories:** Generalised states include motor trajectories, which
  implicitly entail intentions (future motor states). Optimizing the representation of
  these intentions enables prospective predictive coding.
- **Formal connection:** Active inference is formally related to predictive coding.

## Methods

- Neuronal simulations of handwriting generation and recognition
- Synthetic electrophysiological responses to violations of intentional expectations
- Demonstrates itinerant motor trajectory generation and recognition

## Relevance to Our Research

Critical for understanding how active inference handles social cognition and
sensorimotor contingencies. The insight that the same generative model handles
both action production and action recognition is relevant to agent architectures
that must both act and model other agents.

- Sensorimotor contingencies: same model for doing and observing
- Predictive coding as the computational mechanism
- Precision/attention as the switch between agent modes
