# Computation, Cognition and Constructivism: Introduction to the Special Issue

**Authors:** Alexander Riegler, John Stewart, Tom Ziemke
**Year:** 2013
**Journal:** Constructivist Foundations, Vol. 9, No. 1, pp. 1-6
**URL:** https://constructivist.info/9/1/001.riegler
**Type:** Special issue introduction (target article format with commentaries)

## Context

Introduction to a special issue on "Computational Approaches to Constructivism" — a landmark issue for the journal that directly addresses whether constructivist concepts can be turned into computational implementations.

## Key Arguments

- Most constructivist discourse operates at the philosophical-conceptual level; formal-computational models have been taken into account only to a very limited degree.
- The concept of "computational model" is **ambiguous** from a constructivist standpoint: it depends on whether computation is used in the sense of the computational theory of mind (representationalist) or simply as a tool.
- The special issue aims to set "computational constructivism" in motion by presenting computational models of constructivist concepts such as sensorimotor constructions, second-order cybernetic processes, and autopoietic systems.
- Points toward both computational extensions of constructivism AND constructivist critiques of computational approaches (bidirectional).

## Relevance to Project

This is the single most relevant issue of CF for our research. It directly asks: can constructivism be implemented computationally? The tension between constructivism's anti-representationalism and computation's dependence on representations is exactly the productive tension we need to understand for agent design.
