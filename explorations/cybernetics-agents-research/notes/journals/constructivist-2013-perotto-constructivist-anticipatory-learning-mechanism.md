# A Computational Constructivist Model as an Anticipatory Learning Mechanism for Coupled Agent-Environment Systems

- **Author:** Filipo Studzinski Perotto
- **Journal:** Constructivist Foundations 9(1): 46–56
- **Year:** 2013
- **URL:** https://constructivist.info/9/1/046.perotto
- **Open Access:** Yes
- **Special Issue:** Computational Approaches to Constructivism

## Summary

Perotto presents CALM (Constructivist Anticipatory Learning Mechanism), a concrete computational implementation of constructivist principles for agent learning. The agent operates in partially deterministic, partially observable environments and builds its own cognitive structures through interaction rather than being pre-programmed with world models.

## Key Innovation: CALM

CALM is an agent learning mechanism based on constructivist AI principles:

- **Sensorimotor level**: Models regularities through direct interaction with the environment. The agent learns patterns of sensation-action-sensation sequences.
- **Abstract level**: Constructs high-level representational concepts from sensorimotor experience. Higher-order patterns emerge from lower-order regularities.

The mechanism generates behavioral knowledge by building internal models of environmental patterns experienced through agent-environment coupling.

## Meta-Architecture: CAES

The Coupled Agent Environment System (CAES) framework defines autonomous agents characterized by:
- **Environmental situatedness**: The agent is embedded in, not separate from, its environment
- **Embodied structure**: Cognition is grounded in sensorimotor interaction
- **Intrinsic motivation**: The agent's goals emerge from its own structure, not from external specification

## Key Distinction from Mainstream AI

In mainstream AI, agents are typically:
- Pre-programmed with world models (or learn representations of a pre-given world)
- Given extrinsic goals and reward functions
- Evaluated against objective performance metrics

CALM agents instead:
- Construct their own world models through interaction
- Develop intrinsic motivations
- Are evaluated by their viability (continued successful operation), not by correspondence to an external ground truth

## Relevance to Our Research

CALM is one of the few concrete computational implementations of constructivist agent principles. It demonstrates that constructivist AI is not just philosophy — it can be built. Key lessons:

1. **Sensorimotor grounding**: Even abstract cognition must be grounded in sensorimotor interaction patterns. This is directly relevant to embodied AI.
2. **Anticipation over reaction**: The agent learns to anticipate, not just react — prediction is the core cognitive operation.
3. **Intrinsic motivation**: Goals emerge from the agent's own structure rather than being externally imposed. This is radical but aligns with autopoietic theory.
4. **Viability over accuracy**: Success is measured by continued operation, not by accuracy of world-representation.

## Limitations

- The implementation is relatively simple compared to modern deep learning agents
- Partially observable, partially deterministic environments are a constrained setting
- The gap between constructivist philosophy and computational implementation remains significant
