## Abstract / Summary

Examines affective architecture issues relating to the generation of expressive facial behavior. Critiques approaches that treat expressive behavior **only** as a mirror of internal state, arguing that facial expressions also function as **social signals**. Demonstrates advantages of combining both perspectives.

Uses the **FAtiMA architecture** to analyze requirements for generating expressive behavior at both reactive and cognitive levels. Proposes generic architectural mechanisms based on:
1. An **explicit mind-body loop**
2. **Theory of Mind (ToM) processing**

Illustrated through a scenario demonstrating the architecture in action.

## Key Concepts

- **Expressions as social signals vs. internal state mirrors**: Traditional affective architectures map internal emotional state → facial expression. But humans also use expressions strategically as communicative acts.
- **Dual function**: Expressions serve BOTH functions — they reflect internal state AND communicate social intent
- **FAtiMA architecture**: Flexible Affective Agent architecture — supports both reactive and deliberative emotional processing
- **Mind-body loop**: Explicit bidirectional connection between cognitive/emotional state and bodily expression
- **Theory of Mind (ToM)**: Agent models the mental/emotional state of its interaction partner to decide what expressions to produce
- **Reactive vs. cognitive levels**: Some expressions are automatic/reactive; others are deliberate/strategic

## Relevance to Cybernetics-Agents Research

Important for the social/communicative dimension of cybernetic agents:
- **Mind-body loop**: Explicitly models the circular causation between internal state and expression — this IS cybernetic circular causality
- **Theory of Mind as a model**: The agent maintains an internal model of the other agent's state — this is a second-order cybernetic system (models of models)
- **Social regulation**: Emotional expressions don't just report state — they regulate the social interaction, influencing the partner's behavior. This is social homeostasis.
- **Dual function connects to allostasis**: Expressions that are strategic (not just reactive) involve prediction about social consequences — predictive social regulation
