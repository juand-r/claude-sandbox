## Abstract/Summary
Argues that cybernetics has evolved beyond servomechanics and simple models of purposive behavior into a body of theory addressing teleological concepts and reductionism. Views natural adaptation, goal-directed behavior, and conscious purpose as varying expressions of circular causal, recursive, and system/environment relationships along a continuum of organized complexity.

Central claim: the cybernetic paradigm may provide a coherent scientific framework joining the full spectrum of explanation from physicochemical to teleological.

## Key Themes
- Teleology rehabilitated through cybernetics (purpose as scientifically respectable)
- Circular causality as alternative to linear reductionism
- Continuum from simple feedback to conscious purpose
- Bridges Rosenblueth/Wiener/Bigelow (1943) "Behavior, Purpose and Teleology" to modern cybernetics

## Relevance to Our Research
Directly relevant to the question of purpose in AI agents. Modern LLM agents are given "goals" but the nature of their goal-directedness is undertheorized. Winburn's framework provides a spectrum:
- Simple feedback control (thermostat-like agents)
- Adaptive goal-seeking (RL agents)
- Purpose with prediction (planning agents)
- Conscious purpose (???)

This maps onto the hierarchy of agent sophistication we're investigating. The cybernetic perspective says purpose is not something you either have or don't -- it's a continuum of increasingly complex circular causal structures.
