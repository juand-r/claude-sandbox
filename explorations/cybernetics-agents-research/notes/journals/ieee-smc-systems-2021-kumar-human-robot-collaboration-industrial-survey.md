# Survey of Human-Robot Collaboration in Industrial Settings: Awareness, Intelligence, and Compliance

**Authors:** Shitij Pravin Kumar, Celal Savur, Ferat Sahin (Rochester Institute of Technology)
**Journal:** IEEE Transactions on Systems, Man, and Cybernetics: Systems, vol. 51, pp. 280-297, 2021
**DOI:** 10.1109/TSMC.2020.3041231

## Summary

Survey addressing the main challenges in human-robot collaboration (HRC) in industrial settings: safety, trust-in-automation, and productivity.

### Conceptual categorization of HRC:
1. **Awareness** - robot's ability to perceive and understand human presence, intent, actions
2. **Intelligence** - decision-making and adaptive behavior in shared workspace
3. **Compliance** - adherence to safety standards (ISO 10218, ISO/TS 15066) and regulatory frameworks

### Key tension identified
- Complete automation: productive but costly, inflexible
- Manual production: flexible but slow, error-prone
- HRC: leverages human dexterity + robot precision, but requires solving trust, safety, coordination

### Safety measures covered
- Speed and separation monitoring
- Hand guiding
- Safety-rated monitored stop
- Power and force limiting

### Technical standards
- ISO 10218 (Industrial robots - Safety requirements)
- ISO/TS 15066 (Collaborative robots)

## Relevance to Our Research

This paper bridges human-machine systems (a core SMC topic) with practical industrial applications. The "awareness, intelligence, compliance" framework maps to cybernetic concepts:
- **Awareness** = sensing/perception in the feedback loop (Wiener's information theory)
- **Intelligence** = variety management and decision-making (Ashby's requisite variety)
- **Compliance** = constraint satisfaction (regulatory homeostat)

The trust-in-automation angle connects to Pask's conversation theory -- trust as an emergent property of ongoing interaction between human and machine "participants."
