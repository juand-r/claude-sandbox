# Kawato & Cortese (2021) - From internal models toward metacognitive AI

**Journal:** Biological Cybernetics, 115(5), 415-430
**Authors:** Mitsuo Kawato, Aurelio Cortese
**DOI:** 10.1007/s00422-021-00904-7
**Year:** 2021
**Access:** Full text accessed via PMC (PMC8551129). Also on arXiv (2109.12798).

## Summary

Proposes a computational neuroscience model of metacognition based on 20+ years of
internal model research. Introduces the Cognitive Reality Monitoring Network (CRMN),
a modular hierarchical reinforcement-learning architecture of parallel generative-inverse
model pairs.

## Key Arguments

### The Learning Enigma
- Animals learn massive-dimensional problems (10^14 synapses) from remarkably few trials.
- Mathematical learning theory says enormous training samples should be necessary.
- The paper proposes metacognition and consciousness as part of the solution.

### CRMN Architecture
- **Parallel conjugate model pairs:** Generative models (forward) paired with inverse models.
- **Responsibility signals:** Computed from mismatches between generative and inverse model
  predictions, plus reward prediction errors.
- **Consciousness as entropy:** Determined by entropy of responsibility signals across all
  pairs. Low entropy = conscious awareness of specific processes.
- **Prefrontal cortex as bottleneck:** PFC orchestrates conscious involvement of model pairs
  in perception and action, functioning as an information bottleneck/autoencoder.

### Model Selection
- "Cognitive prediction error" combines sensory prediction errors, motor command errors,
  and reward prediction errors.
- Modules receiving higher responsibility signals become subjects of metacognitive awareness.
- CRMN selects which generative-inverse model pairs to deploy.

### Empirical Support
- Decoded neurofeedback (DecNef) experiments showed humans learn gigantic RL problems
  within hundreds of trials in two days.
- Higher metacognitive capability correlated with faster learning.
- Brain search space contracted from whole-brain to basal ganglia/PFC regions.

## Key Results / Claims

1. **Unified framework:** CRMN explains blindsight, aphantasia, sense of agency, and
   psychosis through common mismatch mechanisms.
2. **Consciousness definition:** Entropy across responsibility signals.
3. **AI implications:** Architecture could enable metacognition, dimension reduction,
   and few-shot learning in AI systems.
4. **Bridges consciousness theories:** Combines higher-order theories (PFC monitoring)
   with first-order theories (distributed mismatch computation).

## Relevance to Our Research

Highly relevant to agent architecture design:

- **Internal models are central:** The paper traces from Kawato's 1980s/1990s cerebellar
  internal model work (published in Biological Cybernetics) to modern metacognitive AI.
  Internal models remain the core computational unit.
- **Generative-inverse model pairs:** An agent architecture pattern -- pair a world model
  (generative) with a policy (inverse model) and use prediction errors to coordinate.
- **Metacognition for agents:** If agents could monitor their own internal models and
  select appropriate ones based on prediction errors, this could enable more robust
  and adaptive behavior.
- **Few-shot learning connection:** Metacognition + model selection may explain how
  biological systems avoid the sample complexity problem.
- **Modularity:** The modular, hierarchical structure echoes cybernetic principles
  (Ashby's requisite variety, Beer's VSM).

## Historical Note

Kawato's original internal model papers were published in Biological Cybernetics in the
1980s-1990s, making this a 30+ year arc within the same journal from basic sensorimotor
control to metacognitive AI.
