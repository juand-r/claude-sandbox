\part{Analytic Combinatorics from Scratch}

This part develops the core toolkit of analytic combinatorics, assuming no prior exposure. We begin with generating functions as formal algebraic objects (Chapter~1), promote them to analytic functions of a complex variable (Chapter~2), and introduce the symbolic method that translates combinatorial structure directly into generating-function equations (Chapter~3). The payoff comes in Chapters~4 and~5: the Flajolet--Odlyzko transfer theorem extracts precise asymptotics from singularities, and the Chomsky--Sch\"utzenberger theorem shows that unambiguous context-free languages always produce the universal $n^{-3/2}$ exponent. By the end of Part~I, the reader will have a self-contained workflow: specification $\to$ functional equation $\to$ singularity $\to$ asymptotic formula.
